A More In-Depth Background

Before my current endeavours, I was at the start of a career in education. Studying science brought me joy, and it took me some time and some challenging classes to realise that made me an exception, not the rule.

At 23, I started a chapter of self-discovery. Soon after the lockdowns started in 2020, I used the abundance of time to rediscover myself and my values.

I began pouring myself into learning digital illustration. Hundreds of hours of practising flew by.

Having read before about the Flow State, I delved into learning more about how the mind does what it does. About neurotransmitter production, meditation affecting frontal lobe activity and wait, why isn't my brain working the way I want it to?

Then I learned about being neurodivergent, the dopamine reward pathway, and helping my brain work for me, not against me.

Suffice to say, I find that learning is a need.

Rant warning
One big gripe I have is the way we as a society look at knowledge. Everything is divided and compartmentalised. You mean there isn't a meaningful interconnection between all the systems in our world? Within our selves? The atmosphere isn't a vacuum, and we all live in it together. How can we know if we haven't looked?

It's more fun to live in a world where the flow of curiosity isn't restricted by the borders decided for us.
